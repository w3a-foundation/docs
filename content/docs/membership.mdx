# Membership

Membership in the Alliance is **not a marketing relationship**. It is a
contracted, documented IP-licensing event that materially aligns the member
with the Alliance's compounding economics. Members are legally independent
businesses operating under shared substrate, shared IP licensing, shared
distribution rails, and a shared capital pool. The Alliance does not
consolidate member operations and does not represent member operations to
third parties.

## What membership grants you

Every executed-member admission carries five categories of rights:

1. **Substrate access** — access to the licensed substrate at the executed
   LEL / LRL-PR / SCLA terms. The member's chain (or its rollup on the
   W3A L2) is registered as an "Authorized Network" under the LEL
   Descending Chain criterion, which automatically authorises commercial
   use of every Tier-2 module across the substrate.
2. **Distribution access** — access to the Alliance distribution surface:
   the cross-member routing layer, the federated super-app, and the
   partner ecosystem. A member's customers can reach any other member's
   regulated back end without per-pair integration; the cross-routing
   layer handles the jurisdictional plumbing.
3. **Capital access** — access to the Alliance capital pool at documented
   Layer-2 (member operating) terms, with discretionary Layer-3
   (acquisition / spawn) participation when the member's thesis aligns
   with a network-level transaction.
4. **Coordination forum seat** — a seat on the member-coordination forum
   (quarterly cadence; information-sharing rather than decision-making).
5. **Standard representations and warranties** — contractual reps and
   warranties on the substrate's title, the Alliance's IP estate, and
   the partner ecosystem's performance.

## What membership obligates you to

Each member accepts six categories of obligation:

1. **Operate under the executed license terms** — route through the
   Authorized Network; respect the Tier-3 SCLA gate where applicable; do
   not sub-license without the coordination layer's written consent.
2. **Maintain the brand-via-env-override discipline** — substrate
   upgrades must flow cleanly across the network without per-member
   brand-string forks. Brand identity lives in env-overridable config,
   not in product code.
3. **Maintain SOC 2 sub-service organisation complementary controls** —
   where the member is a regulated counterparty in any jurisdiction, the
   member must keep its complementary controls current and pass annual
   audit.
4. **Contribute distribution into the cross-member routing layer** — at
   the standard 50/50-above-documented-costs commercial template terms.
   Members may not unilaterally fork the routing layer or carve out
   bilateral arrangements that bypass the coordination layer's
   accounting.
5. **Respect the clean-room engineering protocol** — any work that
   extends substrate modules must observe the clean-room protocol so
   that derived IP cleanly belongs to W3A Foundation / Hanzo, Inc. as
   appropriate.
6. **Report quarterly into the member-coordination forum** — financial,
   operational, compliance, and roadmap reporting at the cadence and
   format published by the coordination layer.

## Admission as a licensing event

Member admission is structured end-to-end as an IP-licensing event:

<Steps>

<Step>
**Member executes the LEL** covering the Tier-2 modules its product uses.
The Lux Extended License v1.2 is the canonical commercial-use license for
the source-available patent-protected core; the LEL execution simultaneously
grants the commercial-use rights and registers the member's chain as a
Descending Chain.
</Step>

<Step>
**Member executes any required SCLA** covering Tier-3 LRL-PR modules. The
Strategic Commercial License Agreement is the bespoke commercial agreement
for the highest-value primitives where the Alliance retains direct gate
control over commercial use. SCLA terms are negotiated; the LEL is
non-negotiable boilerplate.
</Step>

<Step>
**If taking Hanzo-source modules**, the member executes the parallel
**Hanzo license** covering Hanzo's AI / agent / MCP / ACI / Jin substrate.
The bifurcated licensor posture (W3A Foundation on the substrate side, Hanzo
Inc. on the AI side) means two licenses for the all-in member, but two
streams of licensing revenue and two layers of defensive posture.
</Step>

<Step>
**Member's chain is registered as an Authorized Network** under the LEL
Descending Chain criterion. This is the operative gate: an Alliance-member
chain is automatically authorised; a non-member chain is not.
</Step>

<Step>
**Member's brand integration is registered in the Alliance brand registry**.
The brand-via-env-override discipline means the member's product runs with
the member's brand at every public surface, while the coordination layer
retains visibility into the brand-policy enforcement surface.
</Step>

<Step>
**Member's compliance and clean-room obligations are accepted** under the
relevant policy documents — SOC 2 sub-service organisation responsibility,
clean-room engineering protocol, vendor-management discipline, and the
incident-response posture.
</Step>

<Step>
**Soft launch on the cross-member routing layer**, then GA. The
coordination layer signs off on the soft-launch readiness, executes the
final routing-layer registration, and the member's surface goes live across
the Alliance footprint.
</Step>

</Steps>

## Application

The public membership application surface is at
[w3a.foundation/join](https://w3a.foundation/join). Initial intake is by
introduction or by direct application; every prospective member runs through
a documented diligence process before any license execution.

The diligence covers:

- Regulatory standing in the member's primary jurisdiction(s)
- Cap-table cleanliness and beneficial-ownership disclosure
- Existing IP estate and any conflicting license obligations
- Technical posture and clean-room readiness
- Compliance program maturity
- Commercial fit with the Alliance's thesis and capacity for the
  cross-routing layer

The Alliance is **open-membership-by-posture**: the network admits
additional regulated banks, money transmitters, acquirers, issuers, brokers,
transfer agents, and crypto-asset service providers in each jurisdiction the
Alliance expands into, on the standard partnership-template terms. The
Alliance is not pursuing exclusivity in any single jurisdiction; the network
is stronger with redundant member coverage in each operating geography.

## What membership does **not** mean

- Membership does not transfer member operations to the Alliance.
- Membership does not consolidate member P&L into the Alliance treasury.
- Membership does not transfer member-level customer relationships, brand
  ownership, or regulatory licenses to the Alliance.
- Membership does not commit the member to indefinite cross-subsidisation
  of other members; cross-member subsidisation requires an explicit,
  documented member-stabilisation distribution from the coordination layer.
- Membership does not waive the member's right to its own product roadmap,
  hiring decisions, or capital strategy at the member level.

The Alliance's discipline is to keep coordination narrow and leave
operations to members. The structural value of the network is in the shared
substrate, shared IP, shared distribution, and shared capital — not in
centralising what each member already does well.
