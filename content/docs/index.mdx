# Web3 Alliance

The Web3 Alliance (**W3A**) is a coordinated network of legally independent
businesses operating under shared post-quantum-secure infrastructure, shared
intellectual-property licensing, shared distribution rails, a shared capital
pool, and a single coordination layer. Members are independent businesses
(their operations are not consolidated); the shared substrate, IP,
distribution, and capital are what differentiate the network from a
collection of arms-length vendors.

The thesis of the Alliance is that the **next compounding network of
financial-services franchises can be engineered from day one** rather than
discovered ex post, with a materially better cost stack, a categorically
better privacy model, a structurally better liquidity-provider economics, the
resilience-by-design of a decentralized non-custodial-by-default substrate,
and a post-quantum security posture that no incumbent will match inside the
NIST CNSA-2.0 PQ-by-2035 horizon.

## The federation model

The Alliance is a **federation of independent members**, not a single
corporate holding. The federation shape is a deliberate design choice — it
preserves member sovereignty, distributes regulatory and operational risk
across jurisdictions, and keeps the substrate decentralized at the
infrastructure layer. The coordination layer at W3A Foundation (and its
parallel licensor Hanzo, Inc. on the AI side) holds:

- All Tier-2 (LEL) and Tier-3 (LRL-PR) licensing authority
- All patent ownership and trade-secret records
- The Alliance brand registry and brand-policy enforcement
- The Alliance treasury (Layer-1 capital)
- The substrate engineering team
- The shared compliance posture (clean-room protocol, SOC 2 sub-service
  organisation responsibility, vendor management)

The coordination layer **does not** hold member operations, member-level
customer relationships, member-level product roadmaps, or member-level hiring
decisions. Those remain with each member.

1. **Chartered banking** — private banking, deposit-taking, lending,
   custody, trust services across US, Canada, EU, UK, IOM, and the Horn
   of Africa.

   jurisdiction.
3. **Acquiring banking** — merchant acquiring, card-present, card-not-
   present, and stablecoin-acceptance rails.

5. **Broker-dealer + transfer-agent + ATS** — FINRA BD, SEC TA, SEC ATS in
   the US; FCA / MAS / VARA / CSSF / MiFID II / MiCA equivalents elsewhere.
6. **Asset management** — fund manager and investment adviser across
   regulated jurisdictions (CSSF via Creatrust; US RIA; non-US
   passportable structures).
7. **Crypto-asset service provision** — MiCA, VARA, MAS, FCA registered
   posture; DPT-equivalent where applicable.
8. **Insurance + reinsurance** — selectively, where the member fits the
   risk-transfer mandate (D&O, cyber, custody, parametric agricultural
   insurance for the Horn-of-Africa corridor).

## Why post-quantum

NSA CNSA 2.0 requires PQ migration for national-security systems by 2035.
NIST FIPS 203 (ML-KEM), 204 (ML-DSA), and 205 (SLH-DSA) are finalised. The
cryptographic primitives underneath traditional financial-services
infrastructure — RSA and classical ECDSA — are categorically vulnerable to
cryptographically-relevant quantum computers. The Harvest-Now-Decrypt-Later
threat model is operational today: long-duration custody holdings, sealed
deal documents, vesting schedules, and any cryptographic material held by
custodians or settlement intermediaries is exposed.

The Alliance's substrate is post-quantum-secure by construction (Quasar
dual-finality, Ringtail R-LWE threshold, FHE precompiles, ML-DSA-65 +
Groth16 SNARK validator binding, PQ-secure KMS envelope). The cost of PQ
migration for incumbents is structural; for the Alliance it has already
been paid. Members joining the network inherit the posture without paying
the migration cost.

## Outcome

If the day-zero asset base holds and the architecture is executed cleanly,
the Alliance is targeting a **$100B–$1T-class institution** over a five-to-
seven-year compounding window. The target is not premised on any single
product hitting outsized adoption. It is the aggregate enterprise value of
the federation when each phase clears its respective milestones, with the
structural cost-stack tailwind (an estimated **$141 billion per year** of
quantifiable annual loss removed from comparable incumbent flows), the FHE-
privacy advantage (**$200M–$600M per manager per year** in eliminated
rebalance front-running), and the LP-economics improvement (LPs roughly
double their risk-adjusted yield on volatile pairs; 5–10× on long-tail
volatile-pair segments) all compounding together.
