# Capital Pool

The Alliance funds itself in **three layers**, each with a distinct source,
mandate, and time horizon. The layered design protects every individual
member from being starved during a slow period elsewhere in the network,
and protects the Alliance's coordination layer from any single member's
funding cycle.

## Layer 1 — Substrate and coordination capital

| | |
|---|---|
| **Source** | Strategic investors with multi-cycle horizons; family offices and sovereign-adjacent capital with discretionary mandates; founders' personal equity re-invested into the Alliance holding rather than monetised at prior exits |
| **Mandate** | (i) Substrate engineering, (ii) patent estate prosecution + defence, (iii) coordination-layer operating costs (governance, legal, finance, treasury, compliance, secops), (iv) reserve runway against any single member's shortfall |
| **Time horizon** | Ten-year minimum lockup. Returns distributed through participation in the Alliance's exits rather than current cash distribution |
| **Custodian** | W3A Foundation parent holding (Delaware) with subsidiary structures by jurisdiction. All Layer-1 capital sits in this vehicle; member-level vehicles draw from it on negotiated terms |
| **IRR target** | 25–40% gross IRR over the ten-year horizon, weighted heavily by terminal enterprise value |

Layer 1 is **patient capital** in the deepest sense. The investors at this
layer underwrite the substrate, the IP estate, and the coordination layer
— not any individual member. Their returns are realised at the
coordination-layer exit (public listing or vertical-level transaction),
not at any single member's strategic round.

## Layer 2 — Member operating capital

| | |
|---|---|
| **Source** | Member-level rounds underwritten primarily by the Alliance treasury, supplemented by external venture or strategic capital where it adds value (e.g., a strategic enterprise customer who wants equity exposure to a specific member; a sovereign development bank funding a regional expansion) |
| **Mandate** | Member-level operating costs, marketing, customer acquisition, regional licensing, and product development specific to the member. Each member is responsible for its own P&L; the Alliance does not subsidise underperformance indefinitely |
| **Time horizon** | Standard venture cycle (5–7 years to liquidity event or sustained profitability), with the optionality of an Alliance-internal recapitalisation rather than a forced external exit when external markets are uncooperative |
| **IRR target** | 30–60% per individual member round (standard venture math); portfolio expectation of 25% gross IRR |

Layer 2 sits at the **member**, not at the Alliance. Each member's cap
table is its own. The Alliance treasury participates as a strategic-LP-
class investor on documented terms; the member retains operational control
and brand ownership. The Alliance does not consolidate the member's
financials.

## Layer 3 — Acquisition and spawn capital

| | |
|---|---|
| **Source** | Operating cash flow from Phase-Two financial super-app revenue, supplemented by Alliance treasury and by structured-debt facilities collateralised against the Alliance's IP estate and substrate-revenue stream |
| **Mandate** | Phase-Three transactions: acquisition consideration, spawn-vehicle initial capitalisation, integration cost, and post-close acceleration capital |
| **Time horizon** | Transaction-specific. Each Phase-Three transaction carries its own thesis with a defined IRR target and a defined exit horizon |
| **IRR target** | 25–35% per individual transaction; portfolio expectation of 30% gross IRR |

The Alliance treasury manages the Phase-Three portfolio as a single book.
Each transaction is underwritten on its own thesis (does the target's IP
slot cleanly into the Alliance substrate? does the target's distribution
extend the routing layer? does the target's regulatory licensure expand
the Alliance footprint into a new jurisdiction?). Approved transactions
enter the portfolio; the treasury then allocates from Layer-3 capital to
the transaction's consideration + integration + acceleration buckets.

## Cross-layer rules

The cross-layer discipline is what prevents the capital pool from
degenerating into a single undifferentiated pot:

1. **No round-tripping.** Layer-1 capital does not fund Layer-3
   acquisitions directly. Layer-1 funds substrate, not member operations.
   Layer-3 capital is sourced from Layer-2 cash flow or external
   acquisition facilities, not from substrate reserves.
2. **No cross-subsidisation by default.** Member A's operating cash flow
   does not fund member B's losses unless the coordination layer
   explicitly approves a **member-stabilisation distribution** under
   documented criteria.
3. **All capital is patient.** The Alliance does not take capital that
   demands liquidity inside 24 months. Members who require shorter-cycle
   capital raise it themselves at the member level, against their own
   P&L, without committing the Alliance's larger capital pool.
4. **Layer purity.** A transaction that bridges layers (e.g., a member-
   stabilisation distribution into a member that is otherwise raising
   Layer-2 capital) is documented as a cross-layer transaction with
   board-level visibility, not absorbed into either layer's normal
   accounting.

## Member-stabilisation distribution criteria

The **member-stabilisation distribution** is the Alliance's mechanism for
supporting a member through a temporary funding gap. The criteria are
narrow and documented:

<Steps>

<Step>
**Operational viability** — the member's product-market fit and
operational posture are intact; the funding gap is timing, not
fundamentals.
</Step>

<Step>
**Network value** — the member's continued operation has demonstrable
value to other Alliance members through the routing layer, the
distribution surface, or jurisdictional coverage.
</Step>

<Step>
**Member-level conditions** — the member accepts the coordination-layer
operational conditions attached to the distribution (typical conditions:
monthly financial reporting, hiring freeze, capital-spend cap, board
observer seat).
</Step>

<Step>
**Documented exit path** — the member has a documented path back to
standalone viability (next-round commitment, customer contract pipeline,
strategic-sale process) within a defined window.
</Step>

<Step>
**Board approval** — the coordination layer's board approves the
distribution amount, the conditions, and the exit-path validation.
</Step>

</Steps>

If the member cannot meet the criteria, the alternative is **orderly
wind-down**, not indefinite subsidy. The Alliance's discipline is to
protect the federation from the long-tail cost of carrying members past
their viability point.

## Treasury policy

The Alliance treasury is operated under documented policy that
**prioritises capital preservation over yield optimisation**:

- **Idle cash** is held in money-market instruments, short-duration
  Treasury bills, and investment-grade corporate paper.
- **Crypto-asset treasury** is denominated primarily in dollar-pegged
  stablecoins (USDC, USDT, USDL and the Alliance's own dollar-denominated
  stablecoin family) held in segregated custody under the W3A MPC + KMS
  posture.
- **The treasury does not speculate on the price of the Alliance's own
  native tokens**; member tokens issued under Phase-Three transactions
  are vested in the treasury on the same schedule as employee equity.
- **All treasury custody runs threshold-MPC** (CGGMP21 2-of-3 + Ringtail
  PQ threshold) across geographically and jurisdictionally diverse pods.
  No single signer, no single jurisdiction, no single key.
- **Annual third-party audit** of the treasury under the SOC 2 sub-service
  organisation framework. Audit reports are made available to members on
  the standard NDA terms.

## Growth-capital sources at the alliance level

The Alliance carries structured access to significant growth capital at
the alliance level — **distinct from any individual member's funding
round**. The capital architecture sources from multiple instruments:

- Strategic investors with multi-cycle horizons
- Family offices with discretionary mandates suited to the alliance
  structure
- Sovereign-adjacent capital from member jurisdictions where the
  partnership terms permit
- Committed capital from existing member exits to be reinvested into the
  acquisition + spawn programme
- Structured-debt facilities collateralised against the IP estate and
  substrate-revenue stream

Growth capital is allocated at the coordination layer under documented
allocation rules that prioritise (i) substrate development, (ii)
cross-member distribution programmes, (iii) member acquisition + spawn
activity, and (iv) member runway when single ventures stumble. **No
single member captures or consumes the alliance capital pool; every
member benefits from its existence.**
